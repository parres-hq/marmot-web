Marmot is an open, end to end encrypted protocol that runs on a distributed relay network and stays fast and reliable. Keys define identity, not platforms.

Get Started

Sovereign identity

End to end encryption powered by MLS, with forward secrecy and post-compromise safety. Keys are rotated and refreshed as membership changes, so even if a device is compromised, past messages remain protected and the group can heal itself. Encryption happens at the client, not on servers or relays. Privacy is guaranteed by cryptography, not policy.

Open network

Scales without central server

Designed to stay responsive across many relays. Lightweight messages and efficient state sync keep communication quick. Clients can publish and read from several relays at once, taking the fastest path. If one is slow or down, another carries the traffic.
